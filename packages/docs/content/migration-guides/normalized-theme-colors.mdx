In the process of expanding our family of design systems to include a fourth
brand (CMS.gov), there were places where theme variables were used inconsistently
between brand themes. In some instances, color variables and whole color families
weren't being used and were left over from Sass variables
that pre-dated our design tokens and theming system. We've cleaned up and reorganized these colors to provide a more intentional approach to color.

### Secondary

<ThemeContent onlyThemes={['core']}>

One of the major shifts was the use of green in the `core` and `healthcare` themes
and the creation of the `info` status color. 

In `core` and `healthcare`, green is
used as a secondary brand color (for buttons), but its `secondary` color family had
to be used for the alternate blue found in some components like the info alert. In
order to free up the `secondary` slot for more appropriate use and get rid of
the non-semantic `green` color family in our theme variables, we created a new
category of status color specifically for informational components like info
alerts, badges, and drawer toggle icons.

### Effect of removal of `gray-cool-light`

Because we removed `gray-cool-light`, we had to remap the background hover color for
day-picker buttons to `primary-lightest`. On all but the core theme, this will result
in a slight color shift for the hover background of those day-picker buttons. Previously
the `gray-cool-light` was mapped to `ocean-50` across the board instead of separate
values for each theme. Now that it is in the `primary` family of colors, healthcare
will use `sapphire-50` instead of `ocean-50`, medicare will use `teal-50`, and cmsgov
will use `deepsea-50`.
